<p>Welcome to the <strong>AIME 2019</strong> website.</p>
<p>The AIME 2019 conference will be held in Poznan, Poland on <strong>June 26-29, 2019</strong>.  As previous AIME conferences (from Pavia in 1985 to Vienna in 2017) it will be a unique opportunity to present significant theoretical, methodological and applied results related to the application of <em>artificial intelligence</em> (AI) in medicine.</p>
<p>AIME 2019 will include two invited lectures, full and short papers, tutorials, workshops, and a doctoral consortium.</p>
<p>Please see the <a href="/index.php/program/program-overview">program overview</a> or <a href="/index.php/program/program-details">detailed schedule</a><g class="gr_ gr_110 gr-alert gr_gramm gr_inline_cards gr_run_anim Style replaceWithoutSep" data-gr-id="110" id="110">. </g></p>
<h4>Conference4me Mobile App</h4>
<p>The detailed program has been also published using the <a href="http://conference4me.psnc.pl/en/"><strong>Conference4me </strong></a>mobile app. Please <a href="http://conference4me.psnc.pl/download/">download the app</a> for your platform (iOS, Android), run it, tap on the "hamburger" menu &gt; <em>Get more conferences</em> &gt; <em>Download conferences</em> and choose AIME 2019 from the list. Then, you will have access to the detailed agenda and additional information. You may also check the <a href="http://www.conf4me.com/aime2019">simplified web-based version of the agenda</a>.</p>

<h2><g class="gr_ gr_123 gr-alert gr_gramm gr_inline_cards gr_disable_anim_appear Style replaceWithoutSep" data-gr-id="123" id="123">Student</g> Travel Grants</h2>
<p>We will offer 6-8 travel grants to students who are authors of accepted papers (full or short) and will present their work during the AIME 2019 conference. Each grant will cover travel costs of up to € 450 (if these costs are lower than € 450, then the actual amount will be covered). Preference will be given to students not participating in the Doctoral Consortium (the later will be provided participation grants). Moreover, we will take into account the quality of submitted papers as indicated by the reviews. The submission of applications will be possible until April 30, decisions will be announced in early May, and grants will be awarded during the conference. Applicants should send the title of their accepted paper, a formal proof of their student status and an electronic copy or scan of tickets via e-mail to <a href="mailto:email:szymon.wilk@cs.put.poznan.pl">Szymon Wilk</a>.</p>

<h2 itemprop="headline">
			Call for Papers		</h2>
</div>
<div itemprop="articleBody">
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">The AIME 2019 conference invites all interested researchers to submit original contributions regarding the development of theory, methods, systems, and applications of AI in biomedicine, including the application of <g class="gr_ gr_83 gr-alert gr_gramm gr_inline_cards gr_run_anim Punctuation only-ins replaceWithoutSep" data-gr-id="83" id="83">AI</g> approaches in biomedical informatics, healthcare organization <g class="gr_ gr_84 gr-alert gr_gramm gr_inline_cards gr_run_anim Punctuation only-ins replaceWithoutSep" data-gr-id="84" id="84">and</g> molecular medicine. </span></p>
<h2><span style="font-weight: 400;">Scope</span></h2>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Contributions to theory and methods should present or analyze novel AI theories or methodologies for solving problems in the biomedical field. They may propose </span><span style="font-weight: 400;">new theories and methods or extensions of existing ones. In both cases, the work should demonstrate its utility for solving biomedical problems and highlight its contribution to the underlying theoretical basis. In addition, it should discuss assumptions, limitations <g class="gr_ gr_102 gr-alert gr_gramm gr_inline_cards gr_run_anim Punctuation only-ins replaceWithoutSep" data-gr-id="102" id="102">and</g> novelty with respect to the state of the art.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Contributions addressing systems and applications should describe the development, implementation or evaluation of innovative, AI-based tools and systems in the biomedical application domain. These papers should both link the work to underlying theory, and either <g class="gr_ gr_90 gr-alert gr_spell gr_inline_cards gr_run_anim ContextualSpelling multiReplace" data-gr-id="90" id="90">analyse</g> the potential benefits to solve biomedical problems or present empirical evidence of benefits in clinical practice.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">The scope of the conference includes the following areas:</span></p>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Big data analytics,</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Machine learning, knowledge discovery and data mining,</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Biomedical ontologies and terminologies,</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Biomedical knowledge acquisition and management,</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Knowledge-based reasoning in biomedicine,</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Natural language processing,</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Document classification and information retrieval,</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Bayesian networks, fuzzy logic <g class="gr_ gr_112 gr-alert gr_gramm gr_inline_cards gr_run_anim Punctuation only-ins replaceWithoutSep" data-gr-id="112" id="112">and</g> reasoning under uncertainty,</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Temporal and spatial representation and reasoning,</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Healthcare processes and workflow management,</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Computerized clinical practice guidelines (CPGs) and protocols,</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Biomedical imaging and signal processing,</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Visual analytics in biomedicine,</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Clinical decision support systems (CDSSs),</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Patient engagement support (personal healthcare record),</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">AI solutions for ambient assisted living, telemedicine <g class="gr_ gr_113 gr-alert gr_gramm gr_inline_cards gr_run_anim Punctuation only-ins replaceWithoutSep" data-gr-id="113" id="113">and</g> e-health.</span></li>
</ul>
<h2><span style="font-weight: 400;">Submission</span></h2>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Papers should be submitted to the AIME 2019 Easy Chair Website (</span><a href="https://easychair.org/conferences/?conf=aime2019"><span style="font-weight: 400;">https://easychair.org/conferences/?conf=aime2019</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;">). Authors are asked first to submit an abstract (maximum 250 words), the full paper may be uploaded up to two weeks later (please see Important Dates below).</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">The submission website will be open from October 1, 2018.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">The conference features regular papers in two categories (please note if you submit a paper as a student):</span></p>
<ol>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><i><span style="font-weight: 400;">Full</span></i><span style="font-weight: 400;"> research papers (up to 10 pages)</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><i><span style="font-weight: 400;">Short</span></i><span style="font-weight: 400;"> papers (up to 5 pages) and </span><i><span style="font-weight: 400;">Demo</span></i><span style="font-weight: 400;"> papers (1 page) describing either </span></li>
</ol>
<ul>
<li><span style="font-weight: 400;">a short research project,</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">a demonstration of implemented systems, or</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">late-breaking results (work-in-progress).</span></li>
</ul>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Papers should be formatted according to Springer's LNCS format (see  </span><a href="http://www.Springer.de/comp/lncs/authors.html"><span style="font-weight: 400;">http://www.Springer.de/comp/lncs/authors.html</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;">).</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">All accepted papers will appear in the conference proceedings which will be published as part of Springer's </span><i><span style="font-weight: 400;">Lecture Notes in AI</span></i><span style="font-weight: 400;"> series. In addition, the authors of the best submissions will be invited to expand and refine their papers for possible publication in the </span><i><span style="font-weight: 400;">Artificial Intelligence in Medicine</span></i><span style="font-weight: 400;"> journal (Elsevier).</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Presentations will include long (25 minutes, 20 minutes presentation, 5 minutes discussion) and short presentations (5 minutes presentation, discussion during poster sessions).</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Posters (A1 size, 594mm x 841mm) will be displayed in two series, a poster walk (2 minutes local presentation) will be organized.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Demos of applications are welcome, there will be opportunities for presenting software to individuals and small groups of participants.</span></p>
<h2><span style="font-weight: 400;">Mario Stefanelli and Marco Ramoni Best Paper Awards</span></h2>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">The </span><b>best student paper</b><span style="font-weight: 400;"> will receive an award honoring Mario Stefanelli from the University of Pavia and his accomplishments. Mario Stefanelli was one of the founders of the AIME community, an inspiration to us all, and actively helped in advancing young researchers in our field. The best student paper will be valued by the Program Committee based on the quality of both, the paper and the oral presentation at the conference. It will be awarded <g class="gr_ gr_106 gr-alert gr_gramm gr_inline_cards gr_run_anim Grammar replaceWithoutSep" data-gr-id="106" id="106">with</g> € 250 thanks to the financial support granted by Springer.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">The <b>best paper in bioinformatics</b> will receive an award honoring Marco Ramoni. Marco Ramoni was an outstandingly respected faculty member at Harvard, USA, and contributed to significant advances in the biomedical informatics field. The best paper in bioinformatics will be valued by the Program Committee based on the scientific quality of the paper. It will be also awarded <g class="gr_ gr_96 gr-alert gr_gramm gr_inline_cards gr_run_anim Grammar replaceWithoutSep" data-gr-id="96" id="96">with</g> € 250 thanks to the financial support granted by Springer.</span></p>
<h2><span style="font-weight: 400;">Student Travel Grants</span></h2>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Six travel grants of up to 450 EUR each will be offered for student authors of papers accepted for the main conference. More details on how to apply will be available in late January 2019.</span></p>
