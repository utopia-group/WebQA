<div class="field-content"><p>If you have heart disease you're not
                                                        alone. The American Heart Association (AHA) estimates that
                                                        79,400,000 (1 in 3) American adults are living with
                                                        cardiovascular disease (CVD). The Polyclinic cardiology
                                                        department's physicians specialize in prevention, diagnosis and
                                                        treatment of all diseases of the heart, coronary arteries and
                                                        heart valves.</p>
<p>At The Polyclinic you can rely on our experienced,
                                                            board-certified cardiologists to use the latest
                                                            technological advances available in cardiology today, at our
                                                            clinic and the First Hill, Providence and Issaquah campuses
                                                            of Swedish Medical Center.</p>
<p>Our cardiology services include cardiac wellness and
                                                            prevention as well as diagnosis and treatment of coronary
                                                            heart disease, valve disease, hypertension, CVD, heart
                                                            rhythm conditions, fibromuscular dysplasia (FMD) and
                                                            congestive heart failure. Other services include but are not
                                                            limited to:</p>
<p><strong>Cardiac Intervention</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Coronary artery atherectomy</li>
<li>Coronary artery angioplasty</li>
<li>Stent placement</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Electrocardiography</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>ECG </li>
<li>Cardiac arrhythmia detection and monitoring</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Electrophysiology</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Cardiac electrophysiology studies</li>
<li>Radiofrequency ablation of arrhythmia</li>
<li>Pacemaker placement</li>
<li>Defibrillator placement</li>
</ul>
<ul></ul>
<p><strong>Heart Imaging Studies</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Echocardiography and exercise "stress"
                                                                echocardiography
                                                            </li>
<li>Nuclear studies of the heart muscle, perfusion and
                                                                function
                                                            </li>
<li><a href="diagnostic-imaging/ct-scan/calicum-score">Coronary
                                                                calcium studies</a></li>
<li>
<a href="/diagnostic-imaging/ct-scan/coronary-angiography" target="_blank">CT coronary angiography</a></li>
<li>Diagnostic cardiac catheterization studies</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Subspecialty Clinics</strong></p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/lipid_clinic">Lipid clinic</a> (individualized
                                                                help with lipids management for patients at high risk
                                                                for cardiovascular disease)
                                                            </li>
<li><a href="/anticoagulation_clinic">Anticoagulation
                                                                clinic</a> (individualized help in managing the use of
                                                                blood thinners)
                                                            </li>
<li>Pacemaker and defibrillator clinic</li>
</ul>
<p> </p>
<hr/>
<h2>Would you like to see one of our cardiologists?</h2>
<p>We want your doctor visit as successful and productive as
                                                            possible.  If this is your first time scheduling with a
                                                            cardiologist at The Polyclinic, we will need:</p>
<ul>
<li>A referral from your doctor (primary care provider or
                                                                another specialist)
                                                            </li>
<li>Recent medical records (medical history, scan reports,
                                                                EKG reports, lab work results, and any other medical
                                                                studies)
                                                            </li>
</ul>
<p>To make your appointment most productive, here are a few
                                                            things the Patient Service Representative may ask about when
                                                            you call:</p>
<ul>
<li>Do you have a primary care provider (PCP), here at The
                                                                Polyclinic or elsewhere?
                                                            </li>
<li>Who referred you to The Polyclinic Cardiology?</li>
<li>Have you already received a diagnosis about your
                                                                condition?  If so, what was your diagnosis?
                                                            </li>
<li>What imaging and other medical studies have you had done
                                                                related to this condition?  (You will need to have them
                                                                sent to us prior to your appointment.)
                                                            </li>
<li>Are you currently on any medications or undergoing
                                                                treatment related to this condition?
                                                            </li>
</ul>
<p>Once you’ve collected this information, please call <em>The
                                                            Polyclinic Cardiology</em> at <strong>(206)
                                                            860-2302</strong> to speak to a Patient Service
                                                            Representative.</p>
