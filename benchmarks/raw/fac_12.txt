<p><b><span style="font-size:13.5pt">About Me</span></b></p>
<p>I am an Associate Professor in the <a href="http://www.cs.ucla.edu/">Computer
Science Department</a> of <a href="http://www.ucla.edu/">University of
California, Los Angeles</a>. I worked at Microsoft Research as a Visiting
Researcher in 2017, where I created and led the development of a project that
aims to build an optimizing compiler for multi-lingual data analytical
pipelines, in particular, Microsoft's Scope/Cosmos. I worked at IBM T. J.
Waston Research Center as a Co-op/intern from 2008 to 2011 where I led the
development of a series of runtime bloat detection tools. </p>
<p>My research is in the general area of <b>computer systems</b>,
ranging from programming languages and compilers, to
runtime/operating/distributed systems and computer architecture.  I
develop systems for a variety of applications, including, for example,
data analytics, video analytics, and machine learning. I was the winner 
of the 2018 Dahl-Nygaard Junior Prize, which is awarded each year to a junior researcher that has made promising contributions to the field of object orientation. 
</p>
<p>With <a href="http://www.cs.ucla.edu/~miryung">Miryung Kim</a> and <a href="http://www.cs.ucla.edu/~ravi">Ravi Netravali</a>, I co-direct the <b><a href="http://systems.cs.ucla.edu">Software Systems Laboratory for Data Analytics and Machine Learning (SOLAR)</a></b>  at UCLA.</p>
