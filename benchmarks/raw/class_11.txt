any of the course staff; usernames are given above.
Use <span class="stt">@ccs.neu.edu</span> to reach instructors.</p><p><a href="https://piazza.com/northeastern/Spring2020/cs2510">Piazza</a> is an on-line forum for class
discussions. Anybody can post a question and anybody else can reply. (Please do not post grading
questions to Piazza. It’s best to email the grader instead for those kinds of questions.)</p><p><span style="font-weight: bold">Before posting, check whether or not your question has already been asked.</span> If it already has
been asked and has not yet been answered, provided the question is not more than a day old, it likely
will be answered soon. <span style="font-weight: bold">Do not double post.</span></p><p><span style="color: red">Posting any parts of a solution to any parts of a homework assignment is considered
cheating. <span style="font-weight: bold">You don’t want to do this.</span></span> Limit your answers to other students to providing
clarity when needed and hints when appropriate. This is good practice should you ever want to be a
tutor :)</p><p><span style="font-weight: bold">Note:</span> anonymity on Piazza is implemented to make you anonymous to students but not to staff
members.</p><h4><a name="(part._.General_.Policies)"></a>General Policies</h4><ul><li><p><span style="font-weight: bold">Late assignment policy:</span> An assignment that is one second late is automatically
counted as a full day late. Late submissions will be accepted up to three days late, with a
20% penalty per day per submission. Speak to the professors in <span style="font-style: italic">advance</span> of a deadline if you cannot complete an
assignment on time.  </p></li><li><p><span style="font-weight: bold">Regrade policy:</span> Sometimes mistakes can happen so if you are confused or concerned
about your feedback, don’t be afraid to reach out to a grader for further explanation.
You must submit any requests for regrading at most 7 days after the
homework feedback was released.  </p></li><li><p><span style="font-weight: bold">Laptop policy:</span> No laptops in class.</p></li><li><p><span style="font-weight: bold">Academic honesty:</span> We will strictly enforce Northeastern’s
<a class="plainlink" href="http://www.northeastern.edu/osccr/academic-integrity-policy">academic
integrity policy</a>.  You may discuss problems with other students, but you may
not share or show code to anyone other than your assigned partner or the
course staff.  Violations of academic integrity will be reported to OSCCR and
will have a negative impact on your grade.</p></li></ul><h4><a name="(part._general-lectures)"></a>Lectures</h4><p></p><div class="SIntrapara"><blockquote><p>Sec 2: Mon, Wed and Thurs at 9:15am–10:20am<br/><span class="hspace">        </span>Richards 458<span class="hspace">        </span>Shesh</p></blockquote></div><div class="SIntrapara"><blockquote><p>Sec 3: Mon, Wed and Thurs at 10:30am–11:35am<br/><span class="hspace">        </span>Shillman 135<span class="hspace">        </span>Razzaq</p></blockquote></div><div class="SIntrapara"><blockquote><p>Sec 4: Mon, Wed and Thurs at 1:35pm–2:40pm<br/><span class="hspace">        </span>Shillman 305<span class="hspace">        </span>Razzaq</p></blockquote></div><div class="SIntrapara"><blockquote><p>Sec 5: Mon, Wed and Thurs at 1:35pm–2:40pm<br/><span class="hspace">        </span>Shillman 135<span class="hspace">        </span>Vesely</p></blockquote></div><div class="SIntrapara"><blockquote><p>Sec 6: Mon, Wed and Thurs at 10:30am–11:35am<br/><span class="hspace">        </span>ISEC 102<span class="hspace">        </span>Mihajlovikj</p></blockquote></div><p><span style="font-weight: bold">Lectures policy:</span></p><p>You are required to read the lecture material for the given lecture before coming to class. As with CS2500, the problem sets will include finger exercises and
practice problems, that you are strongly encouraged to look at and confirm that you can solve.  You are welcome to bring solutions to these problems to the course staff during the semester for informal feedback on how you’re doing.</p><p>The graded problems are to be solved collaboratively with your partner when you are assigned one. The problems will consist of structured programming assignments that may be based on the work done in previous weeks, and may also include more creative projects where you can practice your design skills.</p><p><span style="font-weight: bold">Due Dates: <span style="font-style: italic">Thursdays at 9:00pm,</span></span> unless otherwise specified. See the late policy above.</p><p><span style="color: red"><span style="font-weight: bold">Note:</span></span> unlike CS2500 semester, you will submit each problem <span style="font-style: italic">separately</span> on the handin server.  This is for your benefit: the server

problem won’t immediately prevent your other problems from compiling also. Be careful to submit the correct answers to the correct problems.</p><h4><a name="(part._.Pair_.Programming)"></a>Pair Programming</h4><p>You must work on some of the problem sets in pairs. We will assign you a partner. We may switch partners a few times during the semester.</p><p><span style="font-weight: bold">Important</span>
Pair programming means that you and your partner work on the problem sets jointly. You read them together and you work on the solutions together. One of the lab’s purposes is to teach you how to work in pairs effectively; indeed, pairs are provably more effective than
individuals in programming. The rough idea is this: One of you plays <span style="font-style: italic">pilot</span>, the other <span style="font-style: italic">co-pilot</span>. The pilot works on the keyboard and explains aloud what is going on; it is the co-pilot’s responsibility to question everything. After a problem is solved to the satisfaction of both, you <span style="font-style: italic">must</span> switch roles.</p><p>Every partner must be able to solve every homework problem in the end. In other words, you must be able to solve every homework problem on your own.</p><p>If you are having difficulties working with your partner, please inform your lab TA or your instructor quickly: we cannot help if we don’t know there’s a problem.</p><h4><a name="(part._.Academic_.Integrity)"></a>Academic Integrity</h4><p>Any and all submitted work must be your own. If an assignment/lab is to be completed individually, only you and the course staff are allowed to look at your code. If an assignment/lab is to be completed with a partner, only you, your partner and the course staff are allowed to look at your code.</p><p>You are allowed to discuss the problem sets with others, so long as you acknowledge (in comments, in your submitted files) whoever you discussed the problem with. Discussing the "what to solve" is OK, discussing "how to solve" is a slippery slope. Once you start discussing possible solutions, you may not realize when you have crossed the line. Ultimately we will judge you by the work you submit. If you think you are smart enough to use somebody’s code and hide it well enough to deceive us, please channel that intelligence towards completing the assignment yourself!</p><p><span style="font-weight: bold"><span style="color: red">NOTE:</span></span> Be aware that while <span style="font-style: italic">submitting</span> someone else’s code is clearly a violation, so is <span style="font-style: italic">sharing your code with others</span>, even if you
truly just mean to help. You will be doing your friend or peer a disservice by helping them this way. Please direct them to the course staff instead.</p><p>Outside an academic environment, sharing code with unauthorized parties can be a criminal offense and have severe and unanticipated consequences.</p><p>Submitting code that is not your own or sharing your code to unfairly help another student will be considered a violation of the University’s Academic Integrity Policy (page 40 of the <a class="plainlink" href="http://www.northeastern.edu/osccr/wp-content/uploads/2016/06/2016-2017-UG-Handbook.pdf">2016-2017 Undergraduate Student Handbook</a>). Violations of academic integrity will be reported to <a class="plainlink" href="http://www.northeastern.edu/osccr/">OSCCR</a>, and will have strong consequences on your grade, from an automatic zero on the assignment to failing the course.</p><p>If you are ever unsure of whether sharing is unacceptable or not, or you are struggling in the course, please contact one of the course staff.
