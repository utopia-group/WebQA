<h2><a href="/what-we-do/primary-care/">Primary Care</a></h2>
<p>Physicians who practice primary care focus on the general care of patients. They are often the first
                health care provider whom a patient sees. Primary care physicians usually provide continuing care for
                patients over a long time and they often coordinate the specialized care that a patient may receive from
                different medical specialists.</p>

<h3>Insurance Accepted</h3>
<p>Pacific Medical Centers is committed to making it simple for our valued patients to receive the care they
                need by offering a full range of primary and specialty services and accepting most major insurance
                plans.</p>
<p>We accept the following health insurance plans. If you are calling for a Behavioral Health appointment,
                please check with your insurance carrier for benefits and a listing of their participating network
                providers. Always check your coverage with your insurance carrier if you have questions.</p>
<p>It is always a good idea to call your insurance carrier about specific care you might seek or be referred
                to. That way you can learn in advance what your out-of-pocket costs might be. When you call your
                insurance, it is a good idea to:</p>
<p style="margin-left:3em;">- Ask about specific procedure (CPT) codes.<br/>
                - Get a call reference number before hanging up, in case there is any dispute down the road.<br/>

<a name="tips"></a>
<h2>Tips for saving time and getting the most from your medical insurance</h2>
<p>We’ve put together some helpful tips to save you time and help you get the most out of your medical
                insurance. As with everything, good communication is the key to working well with insurance companies.
                Because policies vary, it’s important to contact your insurance company directly to confirm how your
                plan will pay charges for services and to keep records of the conversations.</p>
<p>Prior to calling your insurance company, ask your provider:</p>
<ul>
<li>Is this a routine exam (i.e. an annual exam or physical)?</li>
<li>What procedure (CPT) codes will apply to my visit/course of treatment?</li>
</ul>
<p>When you call your insurance company, have a set of questions ready to ask. Some suggestions are:</p>
<ul>
<li>Do I have benefit coverage for (insert service name/CPT code)?</li>
<li>Is a referral or preauthorization necessary to have this service? (If yes, your primary care
                    provider must initiate the referral or preauthorization.)
                </li>
<li>Do I have a deductible?</li>
<li>Following my deductible, how much will I owe?</li>
<li>How much am I responsible for paying following the insurance coverage?</li>
<li>What is the call reference number for this conversation? (use this in case you need to refer back to
                    the call with another agent)
                </li>
</ul>
<p>To maximize your health insurance benefits, please contact your insurance company's Customer Service
                department for policy and benefit verification. The Customer Service phone number should be located on
                the back of your health insurance ID card.</p>
<p>Please check with your insurance carrier for information on required referrals and authorizations and
                co-payments.</p>
<p>Please bring your insurance card to every appointment.</p>
<p><strong>Questions about the Affordable Healthcare Act?</strong> You can find more information <a href="/help-and-faq/health-benefit-exchanges/">here</a>.</p>
<p>If you are unable to pay your full statement balance, please contact the Pacific Medical Centers Patient
                Accounts department at 206.621.4392 to discuss payment options.</p>
