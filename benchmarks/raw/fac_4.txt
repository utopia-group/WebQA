<b>Biographical Sketch</b>
</td>

<p>

I am an associate professor at the <a href="https://www.cs.utexas.edu/"> computer science department </a> of the <a href="http://www.utexas.edu/"> University of Texas at Austin</a> where I lead the <a href="http://utopia.cs.utexas.edu/"> UToPiA research group </a>. My main research interests are program analysis and verification, program synthesis, and automated logical reasoning. 
I obtained all my degrees (BS, MS, PhD) at <a href="http://www-cs.stanford.edu/"> Stanford University </a>.  I am a 2015 Sloan Fellow and a recipient of an NSF CAREER award.
<br/> <br/>
<a name="research"> </a>
</p><div id="section">
<table border="0" cellpadding="3" cellspacing="0" width="100%">
<tr>
<td width="100%">
<b>Research</b>
</td>
</tr>
</table>
</div>
<p>
My main research interests are static program analysis/verification, program synthesis, and automated logical reasoning.
I am interested in developing  tools and novel techniques to make software systems more secure and reliable. 
In particular, my research focuses on <i> automatically </i> proving the absence of certain classes of errors (e.g., memory safety errors, assertion failures etc.) and security vulnerabilities in software. I am also interested in techniques for automatically synthesizing programs from formal or informal specifications.  <br/> <br/>


You can learn more about my research by checking out my <a href="publications.html"> publications </a> or through  the <a href="http://utopia.cs.utexas.edu/"> UToPiA group web page </a>!




</p>
